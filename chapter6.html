So, we have now two dataframes, one describing rats, one men.

The BPRS-data (shortened B-data) here describes BPRS-scores between two groups of male) Davis (2002). BPRS is a brief psychiatric rating describing the presence of psychiatric symptoms. Each man has been tracked for 8 weeks time, so we have 40 males, 20 treated with treatment 1 and 20 with treatment 2,

The rats-data (shortened R-data) is from (Crowder and Hand, 1990) and describes a nutrition study conducted on three groups of rats,

The three groups were put on different diets, and each animals body weight (grams) was recorded repeatedly (approximately weekly, except in week seven when two recordings were taken) over a 9-week period. The question of most interest is whether the growth profiles of the three groups differ. from Kimmos textbook

Standardisation

Lets now standardize the data and plot it again. So we will create Z-values by following code:
